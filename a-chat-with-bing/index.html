Anomalies are violations of classical symmetries by quantum effects. Discrete symmetries are symmetries that involve transformations that take only a finite number of values. For example, parity, charge conjugation, and time reversal are discrete symmetries that reverse some properties of physical systems. Discrete symmetries can be used to constrain the form of physical laws and to explain some phenomena, such as CP violation and matter-antimatter asymmetry.

Anomalies and discrete symmetries can interact in interesting ways. For example, some discrete symmetries can be anomalous, meaning that they cannot be gauged consistently. This can lead to constraints on the possible values of parameters or couplings in a theory.
